McKaley Nicole Miller, known as McKaley Miller, an American actress and is mainly known for her roles she played in various TV series. Also, she played as Dana Monohan on the television ABC series  “The Gates”, aired in 2010. Also, she became popular for the role played as Rose Hattenbarger on The CW series named “Hart of Dixie”, started from 2011 to 2015.

She was born on 14th May 1996, Texas, USA where she raised and grew up with siblings, and parents. McKaley Miller always credits her parents for being so encouraging and supportive during her career beginning. Also, she found her parents supportive in her acting and dance career as well. She shares strong bonding with her brother.

After completing her studies, she took dance classes, and gradually, she became a competitive dancer. Before joining the acting line, she took part in dance competitions and made her identity as a dancer.

Career

McKaley Miller as a great dancer, initially, started her career with dance. She was a most-talented girl who got training in  Jazz, hip-hop, and other various forms of dance. Also, she worked as a lyrical and ballet for six years.

After joining the film career, she got supporting roles in films and then appeared in “Traveling” and “The Iceman”. In 2012, she worked in a television movie named “I’am Victor” in which she worked alongside Matthew Lillard.

However, her first most notable role was in the television movie named “Inspector Mom” in which she appeared as Danica McKellar. It was released in the year 2006.

Also, she appeared in some independent movies. In 2011, the American actress worked in 3 episodes on the Disney Channel sitcom series named “Wizards of Waverly Place”. In the series, she appeared as Talia alongside Jake T. Austin.

 In 2013, she appeared in “The Iceman” in which she played the role of the daughter of Winona Ryder. In 2014, she worked in the sitcom series named “Partners” in which she appeared as the stepdaughter of Kelsey Grammer.

Also, she appeared as a guest on the MTV series named “Awkward” in which she worked alongside Matty McKibben. While she gave another guest appearance on the television series named “Scream Queens” in which she appeared as Sophia. It was aired in 2015.
