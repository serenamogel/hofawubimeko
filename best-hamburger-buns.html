Best Burger Buns Taste Test

The best hamburger buns are pillowy, squishy, and flavorful. They shouldn’t fall apart or have too big a crumb. Good hamburger buns should also be fairly firm, not too fluffy. We prefer an almost dense consistency that’ll smoosh with the burger patty, fusing the two together. Ideally, the bun should contain a hint of sweetness, too. Most of all, a good hamburger bun doesn’t distract from the star of the show, aka the meat.

We care about your cookout needs. That’s why each year when summer rolls around, we update our burger bun ranking to make sure all of the Grill Dads (a decidedly gender-neutral term) are working with the most up-to-date recommendations. We’ve got some really great finds on here, from classic buns to seeded buns to pretzel buns—and we’ve even included the best gluten free hamburger bun we’ve tried to date. After three separate taste tests, we’re proud of this list and we stand by it. Which buns cracked the top 13? Let’s find out.

Best for a Big BBQ

Ballpark Burger BunsAmazonWalmart

As the best hamburger buns go, these are pretty standard. But the price for an eight-count bag is hard to beat. Whether you go seeded Ballparks or plain, you’re still getting  fluffy, sweet, sizable buns. Say you’re having a party for more than ten people and you just want reliable consistency—this is where Ballpark comes in. If you’re churning out burgers and hot dogs on a grill and are looking for an inoffensive, good quality bun, Ballpark makes the best burger buns for you. These buns make me think of cheap beer and a standard burger, which is never a bad thing. Plus, they won’t cost you $30. —Danny Palumbo

Best Gluten Free Hamburger Buns

Udi Gluten Free Burger BunsAmazonTarget

Even the best gluten free hamburger buns are going to taste a little different to anyone who regularly consumes gluten, and that’s okay. Udi’s gluten free burger buns are a little more grainy and dry than standard burger buns, but the gluten free fam knows the deal. Believe it or not, these are softer, springier, and more moist than everyone’s typical favorite GF bread brand, Schär. With their shiny tops, even proportions, and larger size, they also look more like regular burger buns. I know that might throw some of you for a loop, but it’s true! We were very impressed by these. —Ariana Losch

Best Low Carb Hamburger Buns

Kroger Carbmaster Classic White Hamburger BunsKroger

With a soft, springy texture and no trace of the bitterness you might typically associate with keto bread, Kroger Carbmaster keto-friendly burger buns taste almost too good to be true. They only have 4g of net carbs per serving, but their balanced flavor and squishy texture make them the best low carb hamburger buns by a mile. If you’re cutting carbs, you no longer have to pretend iceberg lettuce counts as a bun—you’re welcome! (Possibly worth noting: Kroger is being sued for allegedly misrepresenting the calorie count in some of its Carbmaster products, so if cal count is important to you, just be forewarned.) —Ariana Losch

Best Bakery Buns

Alfaro’s Artesano Bakery BunsAmazon

These buns look and taste like they’re bakery fresh. They’re fluffy, sweet, and just a little bit doughy. Quite honestly, these satisfied our indulgent, fancy food cravings more than any brioche buns we tried—none of which ended up making our list of the best hamburger buns. These probably aren’t the best burger buns for a plain or meagerly topped burger, but if you’re adding some bacon, sharp cheddar, or flavored mayonnaise, these buns will complement those ingredients well. They’re meaty and doughy in a way that works with a lot of toppings. Buy Alfaro’s when you want to step up your burger game, while not venturing too far into gourmet territory. —Danny Palumbo

Best for a Gourmet Burger

Oroweat Country Potato Sandwich BunsAmazon

These buns are thick and sizable, which makes them the best burger buns if you’re prepping gourmet burgers at home. The potato flavor is great, and they are quite soft, too. Oroweat’s Country Potato hamburger buns are dusted with flour for that bakery-fresh aesthetic, and the sweetness here is really great. Again, these buns are huge and can withstand a whole lot of toppings. If you love a fried egg on top of your burger, these would hold that slippery yolkiness well.
