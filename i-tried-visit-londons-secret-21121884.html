'I tried to visit London's secret Thames beach and it ended in utter misery' - Tom Haynes

Everyone loves the beach - but sadly London is not a coastal town so if you want white sandy strips your only option is to hop on a train to somewhere like Kent or Brighton for the day.

Or do you?

Apparently, tucked away near Tower Bridge on the south side of Thames Path, is a thin strip of beach known as Bermondsey Beach because, well, it's about five minutes from Bermondsey Underground station.

I live in Bermondsey!

And I run by Thames Path all the time, so I figured why not go and pretend I'm on a beach in Ibiza without having to plead with Boris for a Covid passport.

Off I toddled to the river, grabbing a posh lemonade for the occasion - this was going to be hash tag epic. Who needs Brighton, am I right?

I arrived at the path at around 11am, following Google Maps to the water's edge, ready to get my beach on.

The view was great, the Shard and the Gherkin peered at me from over the river in the bright sunshine.

The lemonade was delectable.

But where was the beach?

As I approached the place where Bermondsey Beach supposedly lay waiting for me I realised I had made a crucial error in judgement.

You see, apparently, there's this thing called "the tides" it causes the water level to rise and fall throughout the day - and when I arrived it appeared to be high tide, as you'll see below.

Yup. My beautiful beach was submerged in stinky rank Thames water.

Google tells me the moon was responsible - very rude of her, imo.

As I walked around, I could see a set of stairs leading down to what presumably would have been a beach at 6am, but in this case it simply led straight into the putrid green river.

You could argue that it's my fault for going to the beach at 11am in the first place when it would inevitably be high tide, but if there's one thing I'm not doing it's getting up at 5.30am to go beach-hunting.

This was meant to be relaxing.

Also, not being funny, but the idea of a beach that is regularly soaked in water from the River Thames - famously one of the most toxic bodies of water literally ever - is actually really gross.

Instead, I wandered up and down the Path, which despite not being beach is still lovely.

While I was there I decided to snap some pics of the best parts of the path - namely The Angel, a nice pub which site just on the edge of the river and which sells pints you can take outside and sip while gazing across the water.

Does the beach have pints? No. It just has smelly Thames-soaked sand.

The Path itself offers a lovely river-adjacent walk from Bermondsey to London Bridge, and there are numerous points at which you can stop and snap gorgeous pics of the Shard or Tower Bridge.

ou can then, if you like, walk back through Bermondsey Street and pick up some ice cream or visit an art gallery.

Can you do that at a beach? No.

If you don't fancy a long walk you can - as I did - take a stroll back through the modest but nonetheless beautiful Southwark Park, which doesn't smell of sewage.

Here you'll find wide expanses of verdant paradise both in and out of shade, ice cream vans, and a perfectly serviceable cafe.

As I made my way home through the park, I realised I didn't actually want to visit the beach in the first place - I just wanted to be outside.

And where better to spend the day than in the natural beauty of Southwark Park? The moon and its stupid tides can't get me here.

Returning home I began to appreciate what I do have as a South Londoner and how little I needed the beach in the first place.

Ultimately, if you want to chill out by the Thames, you can do so from the safety of Thames Path, where you don't have to breathe in the presumably foul-smelling odours of the river.

My abortive quest to find a beach may have ended in failure, but it taught me that you don't need a beach to enjoy the sunshine in London.
