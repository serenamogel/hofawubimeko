Brendan Schaubs Net Worth : Lifestyle &amp; Career

An American former professional mixed martial arts fighter,  podcast host and stand-up comedian, Brendan Schaub’s net worth is $2 million.

The American fighter started his MMA career in 2009 after signing to compete on The Ultimate Fighter.

He continued the career of MMA fighter for 5 years until 2014.

Before MMA, Schaub was a football athlete. During his junior years, he had been transferred to the University of Colorado to play football full-time but unfortunately got redshirted in his first season.

After college, Schaub was signed in by the Buffalo Bills to their practice squad but released before the regular season. He then went on to sign with the Arena Football League’s Utah Blaze.

Schaub retired from football in 2007.

The athlete started as a boxer. He initially competed in amateur boxing and won the Colorado Novice-Division Golden Gloves heavyweight title.

Later on, he started training in Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) at Grudge Training Center, where he met UFC heavyweight contender Shane Carwin. The two eventually became friends as well as training partners.

Schaub’s professional MMA career started with a 4-0 record and won the heavyweight title for the Ring of Fire.

He played 15 matches in his MMA career, where he won 10 matches and lost 5.

Currently, he is famous as a podcaster along with being a standup comedian. Moreover, he is known for his podcast, “The Fighter and the Kid.”

Schaub and comedian Bryan Callen started their podcast “The Fighter and The Kid” in 2013. Fox Network produced the show until 2016 where a contract dispute arose.

Recently, Schaub is a solo comedian.

Brendan Schaub: Career

Early Career

Brendan’s career started as a football player in his high school and college years. He opted for a different path once he retired from the sport and started boxing training.

Young Schaub won his first amateur boxing title in the Colorado novice-division Golden Gloves Heavyweight Title.

Later on, he began his professional career in Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) with a 4-0 record and won the heavyweight title for The Ring of Fire.

Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC)

In 2009, Schaub signed in the UFC to compete on The Ultimate Fighter season. He gained victory till the finale when he faced Roy Nelson and was knocked out by punch in the first round.

On March 19, 2011, Schaub competed against Mirko Cro Cop, which he won via a third-round knockout punch. He was awarded Knockout of the Night.

In December 2014, Joe Rogan (A commentator from UFC and Schaub’s friend) had a joint podcast with Schaub and Bryan Callen when Callen and Rogan advised Schaub to retire from MMA.

In 2015, Schaub announced his retirement from MMA on The Joe Rogan Experience podcast.

The podcast and Stand-up comedy

In 2013, Schaub and Bryan Callen started their first podcast, “The Fighter and The Kid,” based on UFC. FOX Network produced the Podcast till 2016.

They also had started their tour to perform shows that were a mix between live podcasts and a duo stand-up comedy.

Moreover, in 2016, Schaub launched his solo podcast titled Big Brown Breakdown related to combat sports and mixed martial arts. The episodes air on YouTube.

However, the show generated controversy when he claimed that the UFC on the Fox panel of presenters had been selected based on race to show diversity.

Schaub and Callen also released a sketch comedy series on iTunes named The Fighter and The Kid 3D during the year 2016.

In February 2018, Schaub partnered with Showtime Television Network to rebrand his solo podcast to the name “Below the Belt with Brendan Schaub.”

Simultaneously, they released a web show of the same name where Schaub would serve as the host.

In December 2018, Schaub and Theo Von announced their new podcast titled “King and the Sting.”
